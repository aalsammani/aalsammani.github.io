Abdallah Alsammani, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor of Mathematics and Data Science

Department of Mathematical Sciences

Delaware State University

Dover, Delaware 19901, USA

I am an applied mathematician and data scientist whose work develops mathematical models and machine-learning methods for complex biological, clinical, and public-health systems. My research integrates dynamical systems (ODEs and SDEs), optimal control, and statistical learning to study infectious-disease dynamics, build interpretable clinical-prediction tools, and analyze neural and electrophysiological data, including intracranial EEG.

I earned my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from Auburn University, held postdoctoral appointments at the University of Nebraska Medical Center and the University of Georgia, and served as Assistant Professor of Data Science at Jacksonville University before joining Delaware State University in 2025. I welcome interdisciplinary collaboration across public health, medicine, neuroscience, and other data-driven domains.
