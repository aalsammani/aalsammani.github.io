Lab

Applied Mathematics & Data Science Lab

The AMDS Lab at Delaware State University develops mathematical models and data-driven methods for problems in public health, biomedical science, and computational neuroscience. We are a collaborative, interdisciplinary group committed to rigorous research and the training of the next generation of applied mathematicians and data scientists.

Principal Investigator
Abdallah Alsammani, Ph.D.
Assistant Professor of Mathematics and Data Science
Department of Mathematical Sciences, Delaware State University

Dr. Alsammani is an applied mathematician and data scientist with over a decade of combined research and teaching experience. His research develops mathematical models, optimal control methods, and machine learning techniques for infectious disease dynamics, computational neuroscience, and clinical prediction. He received his Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from Auburn University in 2020, and held postdoctoral appointments at the University of Nebraska Medical Center and the University of Georgia before joining Delaware State University in 2025.

Join the Lab

I welcome applications from motivated graduate and undergraduate students with backgrounds in mathematics, statistics, computer science, or related quantitative disciplines. Students with interests in mathematical modeling, machine learning, epidemiology, or computational neuroscience are especially encouraged to reach out.

Prospective Ph.D. and M.S. applicants should apply through the Delaware State University graduate program in Mathematical Sciences and contact me directly to discuss research opportunities.
