About Me

I am a dedicated and interdisciplinary mathematician and data scientist with over nine years of teaching experience, strong quantitative modeling skills, and a proven track record in cross-functional research.

Currently, I serve as an Assistant Professor of Data Science (Tenure-track) at Jacksonville University, where I design and teach courses in Data Science and Mathematics, mentor undergraduate research and collaborate on interdisciplinary projects.

My primary research areas include mathematical modeling, applied statistics, data science, and machine learning, especially as these fields intersect with biology, medicine, economics, and technology.

I received my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from Auburn University. Before joining Jacksonville University, I served as a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Georgia and the University of Nebraska Medical Center, focusing on epidemiological modeling and computational neuroscience.
