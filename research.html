Research Overview

I develop mathematical and computational tools to study complex biological systems. My research combines differential equations, optimal control theory, and statistical learning to model the spread and control of infectious diseases such as COVID-19, cholera, and hepatitis B. A second line of work uses machine learning to extract clinically useful predictions from biomedical datasets, including sleep disorder classification and neural signal analysis. I am also interested in novel statistical methods, particularly circular statistics with applications in computational neuroscience.
