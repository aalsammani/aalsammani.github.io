Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of applied mathematics, data science, statistics, and machine learning, focusing on understanding and influencing biological, ecological, and medical systems. By uniting mathematical theory, computational methods, and data-driven approaches, I develop rigorous models that reveal patterns, predict behaviors, and guide real-world decision-making. Synergistic approaches—combining analytical models with high-quality data and advanced computational techniques—drive my work to tackle challenges that single-method strategies cannot.

Primary Research Areas

Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Foundations

Specializing in dynamical systems and differential equations, I use these tools to model infectious diseases, oncological processes, and ecological networks. Through stability analysis and sensitivity studies, I uncover conditions that influence disease persistence, treatment outcomes, and population dynamics. My doctoral research on stochastic and nonautonomous hepatitis B virus (HBV) models provided critical insights into parameters governing disease spread and persistence.

Computational and Data-Driven Science

Leveraging large-scale biological and clinical datasets, I apply machine learning and advanced data visualization techniques to problems in epidemiology, neuroscience, and healthcare analytics. Recent projects include using natural language processing for clinical outcome prediction and analyzing time-series intracranial EEG data to identify neurological patterns. These efforts extract actionable insights from complex, multidimensional data.

Interdisciplinary and Ethical Dimensions

My research extends to practical applications in neuroscience, epidemiology, environmental science, and business analytics. I focus on developing evidence-based models for disease control, personalized medicine, and policy-making. Aware of the ethical implications of AI and machine learning, I am committed to promoting transparency, fairness, and responsible decision-making in all my research endeavors.

Current Research Projects

  • Mathematical Epidemiology: Collaborating with the University of Florida to model COVID-19 vaccination strategies, exploring the interplay between vaccination rates, economic factors, and public health outcomes.
  • Data-Driven Healthcare Analytics: Developing machine learning algorithms, including NLP-based models, to analyze EHRs from resources like MIMIC-IV. This work enhances patient care by predicting outcomes and refining treatment methodologies.
  • Data Science Education: Investigating strategies for teaching data science, emphasizing real-world problem-solving, coding proficiency, and ethical considerations in curricula.

Future Directions

Advancing Computational Models

I aim to develop multi-scale models that integrate molecular dynamics with population-level data, addressing complex challenges such as cancer tumor growth, vector-borne disease spread, and ecological network resilience under environmental stressors.

Integrating AI in Healthcare

By combining cutting-edge machine learning techniques with rigorous mathematical modeling, I strive to enhance risk stratification, early detection, and treatment optimization while maintaining ethical transparency and fairness.

Building Collaborative Research Environments

My vision includes creating an interdisciplinary research lab that fosters collaboration among mathematicians, clinicians, biologists, and industry partners. This initiative aims to pursue grant-supported projects and mentor emerging researchers.
