Research Overview

My research is interdisciplinary, integrating applied mathematics, biostatistics, data science, and machine learning to study complex biological, clinical, and public-health systems. I develop mathematically rigorous and computationally efficient approaches that bridge theory, data, and real-world applications in healthcare and the biomedical sciences, spanning mathematical epidemiology, machine learning for healthcare, scientific machine learning, and computational neuroscience.

Recent work includes coupled epidemiological–economic models of COVID-19, interpretable and calibrated machine learning for ICU mortality and mycetoma prognosis, bias-corrected circular statistics for intracranial EEG, adaptive control of fractional-order systems, and symbolic structural-identifiability analysis of ODE models. I work to connect mechanistic modeling with modern data-driven inference, prediction, and control.

Focus Areas

Research Areas

Six interconnected lines of inquiry, each grounded in mathematical theory and directed toward problems in public health, biomedicine, and the data sciences.

Mathematical & Computational Epidemiology

Deterministic and stochastic models of infectious diseases including hepatitis B, COVID-19, and cholera. I study stability, identifiability, and the design of vaccination, treatment, and sanitation strategies using bifurcation analysis and optimal control.

ODE / SDE Systems Stability & Bifurcation Optimal Control R₀ Analysis

Machine Learning for Healthcare

Predictive models for clinical outcomes and disease risk, with emphasis on interpretability, calibration, and data-driven decision support. Recent projects span ICU mortality prediction, mycetoma prognosis, and sleep-disorder classification.

Interpretable ML Calibration EHR Data Clinical Prediction

Scientific Machine Learning

Physics-informed and data-driven methods for dynamical systems, including neural differential equations, hybrid mechanistic–statistical modeling, and symbolic structural-identifiability analysis for inference, prediction, and control.

Physics-Informed ML Neural ODEs Identifiability Hybrid Models

Computational Neuroscience

Statistical and computational analysis of electrophysiological data, including detection and characterization of high-frequency oscillations in epilepsy and their dependence on sleep, vigilance state, and recording duration in long-term intracranial EEG.

Intracranial EEG HFO Detection Epilepsy Phase Analysis

Statistical Methodology

Robust statistical techniques for biomedical and high-dimensional data, including circular statistics, bias correction, survival and time-series analysis, and explainable machine learning applied to public-safety and health datasets.

Circular Statistics Bias Correction Explainable ML Bayesian Inference

Systems, Multiscale & Data Systems

Modeling of biological systems across scales — host–pathogen dynamics, ecological interactions, and coupled within-host/population processes — alongside collaborative work on scalable, consistent NoSQL and document-oriented data systems.

Multiscale Modeling Host–Pathogen Fractional Calculus NoSQL

Looking Ahead

Future Research Directions

Ongoing and planned work extending current research at the intersection of applied mathematics, machine learning, and biomedical science.

01

AI-Informed Epidemic Forecasting

Combining mechanistic compartmental models with machine learning to build hybrid forecasting systems that encode biological structure while learning from real-time surveillance data.

02

Trustworthy Clinical AI

Calibration, uncertainty quantification, and interpretability for clinical prediction — from ICU outcomes to multimodal EHR models — with attention to fairness and health disparities.

03

Scientific Machine Learning for Dynamical Systems

Neural differential equations, physics-informed learning, and structural-identifiability analysis for inference and control of mechanistic biological models.

04

HFO Detection in Epilepsy

Deep-learning architectures for automated detection and classification of pathological high-frequency oscillations in long-term intracranial EEG, building on ongoing clinical collaborations.
